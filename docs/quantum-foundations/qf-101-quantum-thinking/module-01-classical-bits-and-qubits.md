# Module 1: Classical Bits and Qubits

## Purpose

This module introduces the difference between a classical bit and a qubit. The goal is not to make qubits sound mysterious. The goal is to use careful language from the beginning.

## Classical Bits

A classical bit is the basic unit of classical digital information. When a classical bit is read, it has one of two values:

- `0`
- `1`

Classical computers build everything from these kinds of definite states. A file, a number, an image, or a message can all be represented using many classical bits arranged in structured ways.

## Qubits

A qubit is a unit of quantum information. When a qubit is measured in the standard computational basis, the measurement outcome is still reported as one of two classical results:

- `0`
- `1`

The important difference is that the qubit's state before measurement is not always the same kind of definite value as a classical bit. Before measurement, a qubit can be prepared in a quantum state that gives different probabilities for possible measurement outcomes.

## State Before Measurement

When we talk about a qubit before measurement, we are talking about the quantum state used to predict measurement outcomes. The state is not simply the final answer waiting to be uncovered.

This is why quantum experiments often need repeated measurements. A single measurement gives one observed outcome. Many repeated measurements help us estimate the pattern of outcomes.

## Measurement Outcome

After measurement, the result you record is classical. In a simple one-qubit measurement, you record either `0` or `1`.

That does not mean the qubit was just a normal classical bit all along. The measurement result is a classical outcome produced from a quantum experiment.

## Why Qubits Are Not Simply Better Bits

Qubits are not automatically faster, larger, or more powerful versions of classical bits. They follow different rules. Quantum advantage depends on the problem, the algorithm, the hardware or simulator, and the way information is prepared and measured.

A careful beginner statement is:

> A qubit is quantum information that can produce classical measurement outcomes according to the probabilities of its quantum state.

## Vocabulary

- **Classical bit**: A unit of classical information with value `0` or `1`.
- **Qubit**: A unit of quantum information.
- **Measurement**: The process that produces a classical result from a quantum system.
- **Outcome**: The recorded result of a measurement.
