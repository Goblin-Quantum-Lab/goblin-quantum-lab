# QF-101 Competency Check

This student-facing competency check helps learners review QF-101 concepts. It includes multiple-choice questions, short-answer prompts, a result-interpretation task, and a beginner report task.

Official solutions and official grading logic are not published in the public repository. Badge/completion review is manual. Review may take up to 30 business days.

Do not submit private personal data, credentials, secrets, API keys, assessment solutions, or protected grading material in public issues or pull requests.

## Multiple-Choice Questions

Choose one option for each question.

1. What is a classical bit?
   - A. A value that is always both `0` and `1`
   - B. A unit of classical information with value `0` or `1`
   - C. A quantum measurement device
   - D. A histogram of experiment results

2. What is a qubit?
   - A. A unit of quantum information
   - B. A faster classical bit
   - C. A finished lab report
   - D. A private scoring artifact

3. What does a one-qubit measurement usually produce in the computational basis?
   - A. A paragraph
   - B. A classical outcome such as `0` or `1`
   - C. A guaranteed balanced histogram
   - D. A formal certificate

4. Why are repeated shots used in quantum experiments?
   - A. To collect a pattern of outcomes
   - B. To hide the result
   - C. To avoid writing observations
   - D. To make every run identical

5. What is a count?
   - A. The number of times an outcome appears
   - B. The name of a quantum gate
   - C. A private learner record
   - D. A proof that a simulator is perfect

6. What does a distribution describe?
   - A. How outcomes are spread across possible results
   - B. A legal credential
   - C. A private grading script
   - D. A protected assessment check

7. Which statement is the most careful beginner description of superposition?
   - A. A qubit is magic
   - B. A qubit is only a classical bit in disguise
   - C. A pre-measurement quantum state can assign probabilities to possible outcomes
   - D. A qubit always sends messages instantly

8. What is a Hadamard gate commonly used for in beginner examples?
   - A. Creating a balanced measurement pattern from a simple starting state
   - B. Deleting all measurement results
   - C. Writing official grading logic
   - D. Making classical computers obsolete

9. What is sampling variation?
   - A. Natural variation caused by finite samples
   - B. A type of badge
   - C. A guarantee that every run is identical
   - D. A private repository

10. Why can 10 shots look different from 1,000 shots?
    - A. Smaller samples can fluctuate more visibly
    - B. Larger samples are not allowed
    - C. Shots are unrelated to measurement
    - D. The result table is always wrong

11. Which claim should a beginner avoid?
    - A. "The result is consistent with my expectation."
    - B. "The counts suggest a pattern."
    - C. "This simple result proves quantum computers are faster at everything."
    - D. "A limitation is the small shot count."

12. What should an experiment objective do?
    - A. State what the experiment is trying to observe or test
    - B. Reveal private scoring materials
    - C. Replace the result table
    - D. Guarantee badge approval

13. What belongs in an experiment limitation?
    - A. A factor that affects what the experiment can show
    - B. A private API key
    - C. A legal credential claim
    - D. A protected assessment item

14. Which is a public-safe help request?
    - A. A setup question with non-sensitive error output
    - B. A pasted API token
    - C. A private learner record
    - D. A request for private scoring materials

15. What are Goblin Quantum Lab badges?
    - A. Local learning milestones
    - B. Accredited qualifications
    - C. University credits
    - D. Professional licences

## Short-Answer Questions

Answer in your own words.

1. Explain the difference between a classical bit and a qubit.
2. Why does quantum measurement need careful statistical interpretation?
3. Explain superposition without relying only on the phrase "both 0 and 1."
4. Why can small shot counts be misleading?
5. Name one common quantum misconception and correct it in plain language.

## Result-Interpretation Task

Consider this measurement-result table from a beginner one-qubit experiment:

| Outcome | Count |
| --- | ---: |
| `0` | 47 |
| `1` | 53 |

Write a short interpretation that explains:

- What the result appears to support
- What it does not prove
- One limitation of the result

## Beginner Report Task

Write a short beginner observation report for either:

- Lab 001: Single-Qubit Measurement
- Lab 002: Shot Count Observation

Your report should include:

- Objective
- Expected result
- Method
- Execution setting
- Observed result
- Interpretation
- Limitation
- Conclusion

Use the [Beginner Lab Report Template](/docs/report-writing/beginner-lab-report-template) if helpful.
