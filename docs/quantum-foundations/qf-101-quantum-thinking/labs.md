# QF-101 Labs

QF-101 uses two beginner labs. These labs are designed to build observation discipline, not advanced programming skill.

## Lab 001: Single-Qubit Measurement

Purpose: Introduce learners to a simple prepared quantum state and its measurement result.

Learner task:

- Inspect or run a simple one-qubit measurement example.
- Record the expected result.
- Record the observed result.
- Explain whether the observed result matches expectation.
- State one limitation of the experiment.

Open the full lab: [Lab 001: Single-Qubit Measurement](/docs/labs/beginner/lab-001-single-qubit-measurement)

## Lab 002: Shot Count Observation

Purpose: Show why repeated shots matter.

Learner compares:

- 10 shots
- 100 shots
- 1,000 shots

Learner task:

- Compare the result patterns.
- Explain why small samples may appear unstable.
- Explain why larger shot counts usually provide a clearer distribution.

Open the full lab: [Lab 002: Shot Count Observation](/docs/labs/beginner/lab-002-shot-count-observation)

## Lab Submission Boundary

Public labs do not include official solutions, sample completed responses, protected assessment checks, official grading scripts, or official grading logic.

When asking for help in public, do not post credentials, secrets, private data, or protected assessment material.
