# Module 6: First Experiment Discipline

## Purpose

This module introduces a simple structure for beginner quantum experiment notes. The structure helps learners avoid vague claims and build reproducible habits early.

## Objective

The objective states what the experiment is trying to observe or test.

Example shape:

> The objective of this lab is to observe measurement results from a simple one-qubit experiment.

Your objective should be short, specific, and connected to the lab.

## Expected Result

The expected result is written before inspecting the final counts. It should describe what result pattern you expect and why.

You do not need advanced mathematics. A beginner expectation can be written in plain language.

## Method

The method describes what you did.

Include:

- The circuit or prepared state you inspected
- The measurement step
- The number of shots
- The simulator or tool used, if any

## Execution Setting

The execution setting records where or how the experiment ran.

Examples:

- Local simulator
- Browser-based notebook
- Classroom-provided example
- Public tutorial environment

Do not include private credentials, tokens, API keys, or personal account details.

## Result

The result records the observed counts or table.

Keep this section descriptive. Do not interpret yet.

## Interpretation

The interpretation compares the expected result with the observed result. It should say what the result supports, what it does not prove, and whether sampling variation may matter.

## Limitation

A limitation describes something the experiment cannot show or a factor that affects confidence.

Examples of limitation types:

- Small shot count
- Simulator instead of hardware
- No noise model
- Very simple circuit
- No comparison run

## Conclusion

The conclusion is a short final statement about what you learned from the experiment.

Keep it modest. A beginner one-qubit lab should not claim to prove broad statements about all quantum computing.

Use the structure:

```text
Expected result:
Limitation:
```

Do not include a solution guide.
