# QF-101 Badge Criteria

Goblin Quantum Lab badges are local learning milestones. They are not accredited qualifications, professional licences, university credits, or externally verified credentials.

Badge requests are manually reviewed by maintainers or approved reviewers. Review may take up to 30 business days. Submitting a badge request does not guarantee approval.

## Public Review Boundary

Public badge criteria describe what learners should be able to demonstrate. The public repository does not publish official solutions, sample completed responses, protected assessment checks, official grading scripts, official grading logic, private reviewer notes, or learner records.

## First Qubit

Learner milestone for beginning QF-101.

Public criteria:

- Describes a classical bit as a value of `0` or `1`.
- Describes a qubit as quantum information rather than a better classical bit.
- Explains that a measurement produces a classical outcome.
- Avoids claiming that a qubit simply stores both values like a hidden classical object.

Suggested evidence:

- Completion notes for Module 1.
- A short written explanation from the Module 1 mini task.

## Measurement Ready

Learner milestone for probability, measurement, and repeated shots.

Public criteria:

- Explains what a shot is.
- Reads a simple count table.
- Explains why one measurement is not enough to understand a result pattern.
- Describes why 10 shots and 1,000 shots may look different.

Suggested evidence:

- Completion notes for Module 2.
- Lab 002 comparison notes.

## Superposition Starter

Learner milestone for careful superposition language.

Public criteria:

- Explains superposition using pre-measurement state and possible outcomes.
- Avoids using only the phrase "both 0 and 1."
- Connects superposition to probability and measurement.
- Uses beginner-safe language without hype.

Suggested evidence:

- Completion notes for Module 3.
- A short superposition explanation written in the learner's own words.

## Careful Quantum Thinker

Learner milestone for result interpretation and misconception correction.

Public criteria:

- Interprets a simple result table carefully.
- Distinguishes expected result from observed result.
- Identifies at least one limitation of a beginner experiment.
- Corrects at least one common misconception in plain language.

Suggested evidence:

- Completion notes for Modules 4 and 5.
- A short interpretation of a result table.

## QF-101 Completion

Learner milestone for completing the full QF-101 public course sequence.

Public criteria:

- Completes Modules 1-6.
- Completes Lab 001 or Lab 002.
- Completes the QF-101 competency check prompts.
- Writes a short beginner observation report.
- Follows public-safe submission rules.

Suggested evidence:

- Course notes.
- Lab notes.
- Competency check responses.
- Beginner observation report.

## Review Timing

Badge requests are manually reviewed by maintainers or approved reviewers. Review may take up to 30 business days. Submitting a badge request does not guarantee approval.
