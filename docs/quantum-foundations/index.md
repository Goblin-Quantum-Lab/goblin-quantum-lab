# Quantum Foundations

Welcome to the Quantum Foundations pathway. This section covers the fundamental principles of quantum mechanics and quantum computing, providing the theoretical foundation for all subsequent learning.

## Overview

Quantum Foundations is designed for learners new to quantum computing. It covers essential concepts including quantum mechanics principles, qubits, quantum gates, and basic quantum circuits.

## Learning Objectives

By completing this pathway, you will understand:

- The principles of quantum mechanics relevant to computing
- How qubits differ from classical bits
- Basic quantum gates and their operations
- How to construct simple quantum circuits
- The measurement problem and quantum collapse
- Superposition and entanglement

## Topics

This pathway is organized into the following topics:

### 1. Quantum Mechanics Basics
- Classical vs. quantum systems
- Wave-particle duality
- Quantum superposition
- Quantum measurement

### 2. Qubits and Quantum States
- The qubit: fundamental unit of quantum information
- Quantum state representation
- Bloch sphere visualization
- Multiple qubit systems

### 3. Quantum Gates
- Single-qubit gates (Pauli, Hadamard, Phase)
- Multi-qubit gates (CNOT, SWAP)
- Gate operations and matrix representation
- Universal gate sets

### 4. Quantum Circuits
- Circuit design and notation
- Circuit composition
- Quantum circuit simulation
- Running circuits on simulators

### 5. Quantum Measurement
- Measurement postulate
- Measurement outcomes and probabilities
- Measurement in different bases
- Quantum collapse

### 6. Entanglement and Superposition
- Creating entangled states
- Bell states
- Quantum correlations
- Applications of entanglement
