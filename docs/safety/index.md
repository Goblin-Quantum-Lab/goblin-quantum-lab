# Safety and Experiment Discipline

Welcome to the Safety section of Goblin Quantum Lab. This section is dedicated to ensuring that all learners and contributors maintain safe, ethical, and disciplined practices in their quantum computing work.

## Why Safety Matters

Safety and discipline are fundamental to responsible quantum computing education. They protect both learners and the integrity of the project. By following safety guidelines, you ensure that your work is reproducible, your findings are reliable, and your contributions are valuable to the community.

## Research Ethics

Research ethics are paramount in quantum computing education. This involves **honesty** in reporting results accurately, even failures, and providing proper **attribution** to all sources and collaborators. Maintaining **integrity** means refraining from fabricating or manipulating data. Researchers should consider the broader implications of their work, demonstrating **responsibility**, and always adhere to community standards and guidelines with **respect**.

## Code of Conduct

All participants in Goblin Quantum Lab must follow our Code of Conduct. This includes being respectful and inclusive, avoiding harassment and discrimination, engaging constructively in discussions, reporting violations to maintainers, and assuming good faith in all interactions.

## Reproducibility Standards

To ensure your work can be reproduced by others, it is essential to document everything, including methods, parameters, and software versions. Provide all code and notebooks, specify exact dependencies and library versions, and use random seeds for deterministic results. Where possible, share your data and always document your findings clearly in reports.

## Troubleshooting and Support

If you encounter problems during labs or assessments, first document the issue, noting what happened, when, and how. Attempt to reproduce the problem if possible. Check available resources like documentation and FAQs. If the issue persists, ask for help in the community or open an issue. Security issues should always be reported privately to the maintainers.

## Security Considerations

Never commit passwords or API keys to the repository. Keep all libraries and dependencies updated to address security vulnerabilities. Engage in code review to catch potential issues, and refer to the Security Policy for vulnerability reporting procedures.

## Data and Privacy Protection

Do not submit secrets, API keys, private data, or personal records to the public repository. Do not publish answer keys or hidden grading materials. Report accidental exposure of assessment material as an integrity issue.

## Assessment Integrity

Assessment integrity is critical to maintaining the credibility of Goblin Quantum Lab. Never attempt to access hidden tests, answer keys, or official grading materials. Do not share solutions with other students before assessments are completed. Report any suspected assessment integrity violations to the maintainers.

## Accessibility and Inclusion

We are committed to making Goblin Quantum Lab accessible to all. This involves providing alternative formats for materials, using clear and simple language, including captions and transcripts for multimedia, ensuring tools are accessible, and welcoming diverse perspectives.

## Mental Health and Wellbeing

Learning quantum computing can be challenging. Remember to take breaks when frustrated, avoid comparing your progress to others, seek help when stuck, celebrate small wins, and balance learning with other activities to maintain your mental health and wellbeing.

## Reporting Concerns

To report safety, ethical, or conduct concerns, contact the maintainers privately. Provide details and context, and suggest potential solutions if possible. Respect confidentiality and allow time for investigation and response. Refer to the Security Policy for reporting procedures.

## Resources

For more information, please consult our Code of Conduct, Contributing Guide, Security Policy, and Reproducibility Standards.
