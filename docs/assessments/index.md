# Assessments

Welcome to the Assessments section of Goblin Quantum Lab. This section provides information about how we evaluate learning and maintain assessment integrity.

## What Are Assessments?

Assessments are tools designed to help you verify your understanding of quantum computing concepts and skills. They range from simple competency checks to comprehensive lab evaluations. Assessments serve multiple purposes: they help you identify areas where you need more practice, provide feedback on your progress, and verify that you have mastered key concepts.

## Types of Assessments

Goblin Quantum Lab offers various types of assessments to accommodate different learning styles and objectives:

**Competency Checks** are quick self-assessment tools that help you verify your understanding of specific concepts. They are typically shorter and focus on core knowledge.

**Lab Assessments** evaluate your ability to apply concepts through hands-on quantum computing experiments. These assessments often include code reviews, result analysis, and written explanations.

**Project Assessments** are comprehensive evaluations of larger bodies of work, such as research projects or capstone assignments. They typically involve multiple components and deeper analysis.

**Report Assessments** evaluate your ability to communicate your findings clearly and professionally in written form. These assessments focus on clarity, organization, and scientific rigor.

## Public Assessment Information

This public repository provides information about the types of assessments available, their objectives, and what they evaluate. **Public competency checks may include student-facing questions and instructions**. However, **official solutions and official grading logic are not published**. **Official review materials and learner records are not published in this public repository**.

## Assessment Integrity

Assessment integrity is fundamental to Goblin Quantum Lab. We are committed to fair, honest, and transparent assessment practices. To maintain integrity, sensitive assessment materials and official grading rubrics are not published.

If you discover that sensitive assessment materials have been accidentally exposed, please report this as an **assessment integrity incident** to the maintainers immediately.

## Getting Feedback

After completing an assessment, you will receive feedback from reviewers. This feedback is designed to be constructive and helpful, highlighting your strengths and suggesting areas for improvement. Use this feedback to guide your learning and identify topics that may benefit from additional study.

## Retaking Assessments

Many assessments can be retaken to improve your score or understanding. If you are not satisfied with your initial attempt, you are encouraged to review the feedback, study the relevant material, and try again. Retaking assessments is a normal and encouraged part of the learning process.

## Assessment and Privacy

Your assessment results and personal information are kept private and secure. They are stored only in the private operations repository and are not published publicly. We take learner privacy seriously and handle all personal data in accordance with our privacy policies.

## Questions About Assessments?

If you have questions about a specific assessment, its objectives, or how it will be evaluated, please refer to the assessment's documentation or reach out to the community for support.
