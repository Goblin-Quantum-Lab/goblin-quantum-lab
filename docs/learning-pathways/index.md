# Learning Pathways

Goblin Quantum Lab offers structured learning pathways designed to take you from quantum computing fundamentals to advanced topics. Each pathway combines theory, practical labs, and assessments.

## Recommended Pathways

### Pathway 1: Quantum Foundations (Beginner)

Start here if you're new to quantum computing. This pathway covers:

- Introduction to quantum mechanics
- Qubits and quantum gates
- Quantum circuits
- Basic quantum algorithms

**Duration**: 4-6 weeks  
**Prerequisites**: Basic mathematics and physics knowledge

### Pathway 2: Quantum Programming (Intermediate)

Build practical skills in quantum programming. This pathway includes:

- Quantum simulation frameworks
- Writing quantum programs
- Debugging quantum circuits
- Optimization techniques

**Duration**: 6-8 weeks  
**Prerequisites**: Completion of Quantum Foundations pathway

### Pathway 3: Quantum Algorithms (Advanced)

Explore advanced quantum algorithms and their applications:

- Shor's algorithm
- Grover's algorithm
- Variational quantum algorithms
- Quantum machine learning basics

**Duration**: 8-10 weeks  
**Prerequisites**: Completion of Quantum Programming pathway

### Pathway 4: Research Workflow (Advanced)

Develop skills for quantum computing research:

- Experiment design and planning
- Data collection and analysis
- Report writing and documentation
- Reproducibility and safety discipline

**Duration**: Ongoing  
**Prerequisites**: Completion of at least one intermediate pathway

## How to Use These Pathways

1. **Choose your starting point** based on your background and goals
2. **Work through the material** at your own pace
3. **Complete the labs** to reinforce learning
4. **Take assessments** to verify understanding
5. **Write reports** to develop documentation skills
6. **Progress to the next pathway** when ready

## Flexible Learning

These pathways are flexible. You can:

- Skip sections you already understand
- Return to earlier material for review
- Combine pathways based on your interests
- Contribute your own learning materials
